Punch Log Rules

This guide explains how BizBuddy handles employee time records — when flags and warnings appear on a punch log, what they mean, and what action you may need to take. No technical knowledge required.

Versionv1.0.0
EffectiveMarch 16, 2026
AudienceHR / Supervisors
StatusActive
G-1
Unscheduled Punch
An employee clocked in on a day with no assigned shift
🌐 All Employees 👁 Supervisor Review

When an employee clocks in on a day where they have no assigned work schedule, the system marks the record as unscheduled. This does not block the employee from working — it simply flags the record so you can review it.

When does this happen?

The employee clocked in on a day where no shift has been assigned to them or to their department in the work schedule.

What will you see?

The row is highlighted in amber/yellow with a small warning circle (⚠) next to the employee's name in the Punch Logs table.

What this means for payroll
⏱
Hours are still calculated
The system uses the company's default shift length to determine the hours. The employee is still paid for the time they worked.
👁
Review before approving
Was this an approved extra day? Was the schedule set up correctly for this employee? It's good practice to verify before including this record in payroll.
G-2
Grace Period
A short window where arriving slightly late is still considered on time
🌐 All Employees ⚙️ Automatic

Your company has a configurable grace period — a short buffer after the scheduled start time. If an employee arrives within this window, they are considered on time and no lateness is recorded against them. The default grace period is 15 minutes.

SituationResult
Employee arrives within the grace period
Example: 8:14 AM with a 15-minute grace		 ✅ On time — no late hours recorded
Employee arrives at or beyond the grace period
Example: 8:15 AM with a 15-minute grace		 ⚠️ Late — full delay is recorded and deducted from hours
⚠️ The boundary is exact

Arriving at exactly the grace period limit counts as late. With a 15-minute grace period, arriving exactly 15 minutes after the shift starts is marked late. Only arrivals with fewer than 15 minutes of delay are treated as on time.

Your company's grace period can be adjusted in Company Settings → Time Defaults. Any changes take effect immediately for all new punch records.

G-3
Overtime
How overtime is detected, requested, and approved
🌐 All Employees 👤 Employee Requests 👁 Supervisor Approves

Overtime in BizBuddy is never automatically paid. The employee must submit an overtime request and a supervisor must approve it before any extra hours are credited. Your company's overtime calculation method can be set in Company Settings.

Daily
Most common
Overtime is reviewed for each individual punch. Extra hours beyond the daily limit (default 8 hours) are flagged as potential overtime.
Weekly
Cumulative
Overtime is looked at across all punches in a week. Individual records will show "Weekly basis" — the total is reviewed at the end of the week.
Cutoff Period
Payroll cycle
Overtime is determined when the payroll period closes. Records show "Cutoff basis" and overtime is confirmed during the cutoff review.
What you'll see in the OT Status column
✅
Approved
The employee submitted a request and it was approved. Extra hours will be credited toward their pay.
🟡
Pending
The employee submitted a request that is waiting for supervisor approval.
❌
Rejected
The overtime request was denied. No extra hours are added to the record.
⚠️
No Approval
The system detected potential overtime but no request has been submitted yet. If the extra hours were authorized, the employee should submit a request.
G-6
Auto Clock-Out
The system automatically closes sessions that have been open too long
🌐 All Employees ⚙️ Automatic 🔒 Supervisor Must Review

If an employee forgets to clock out and their session has been running for 20 hours or more, the system will automatically close it. This protects payroll accuracy by preventing forgotten clock-ins from accumulating unlimited hours.

When does this happen?

An employee clocked in but never clocked out, and more than 20 hours have passed since their recorded clock-in time.

What does the system do?

The clock-out is set to the employee's scheduled shift end time — not the time the system detected it. The record is then flagged for your mandatory review.

🔒 This record requires your action

Any record that was automatically closed cannot be submitted for payroll until a supervisor reviews and approves it. You will see the row highlighted in purple with a clock icon (⏱) next to the employee's name.

What to do when you see this flag
🔍
Verify the hours are correct
Check whether the clock-out time the system recorded matches what actually happened. If not, use the Edit button to correct it before approving.
✅
Approve the record
Once you're satisfied the hours are accurate, approve the record so it can move forward to payroll processing.
💬
Follow up with the employee
A gentle reminder to clock out at the end of each shift helps prevent this from happening again.
