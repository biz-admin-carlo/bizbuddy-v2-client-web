Punch Log Rules

This guide explains how BizBuddy handles employee time records — when flags appear, what they mean, and what action is needed from you as a supervisor or HR administrator. No technical knowledge is required.

Version v1.0.0
Effective March 16, 2026
Audience HR / Supervisors
Status Active
📋
General Rules
Rules G-1 through G-6 apply to every employee at every company. These are the foundational time-keeping rules that BizBuddy enforces by default.
G-1
Unscheduled Punch
An employee clocked in on a day with no assigned shift
🌐 All Companies 👁 Supervisor Review

When an employee clocks in on a day where they have no assigned work schedule, the system marks the record as unscheduled. This does not block the employee from working — it simply flags the record so a supervisor can review it.

When does this happen?

The employee clocks in on a day where no shift has been assigned to them individually or to their department in the schedule.

What will you see?

The row is highlighted in amber/yellow and shows a small warning circle (⚠) next to the employee's name.

What this means for payroll
⏱
Hours are still calculated
The system uses the company's default shift length to cap the hours. The employee is still paid for time worked.
👁
Review is recommended
Was this an approved extra day? Was the schedule set up correctly? Review before approving for payroll.
G-2
Grace Period
A short window after shift start where arriving late is still considered on time
🌐 All Companies ⚙️ Automatic

Your company has a configurable grace period — a short buffer after the scheduled start time. If an employee arrives within this window, they are considered on time and no lateness is recorded. The default is 15 minutes.

SituationResult
Employee arrives within the grace period (e.g., 8:14 AM with a 15-min grace) ✅ On time — no late hours recorded
Employee arrives at or beyond the grace period (e.g., 8:15 AM with a 15-min grace) ⚠️ Late — full delay recorded and deducted
⚠️ The boundary is exact

Arriving at exactly the grace period limit counts as late. For example, with a 15-minute grace period, arriving exactly 15 minutes late is marked late. Only arrivals with fewer than 15 minutes of delay are treated as on time.

The grace period is configurable per company in Company Settings → Time Defaults. Changes take effect immediately for all future punch records.

G-3
Overtime
How overtime is detected and approved
🌐 All Companies 👤 Employee Initiated 👁 Supervisor Approves

Overtime is never automatically paid. The employee must submit an overtime request, and a supervisor must approve it before any extra hours are credited. Your company's overtime calculation method is set in Company Settings.

Daily
Most common
Overtime is checked for each individual punch record. Extra hours beyond the daily threshold (default 8h) show as OT.
Weekly
Cumulative
Overtime is calculated across all punches in a week. Individual records will show "Weekly basis" instead of an OT amount — the total is reviewed at week's end.
Cutoff Period
Payroll cycle
Overtime is evaluated when the payroll cutoff period closes. Records show "Cutoff basis" and OT is determined during the cutoff review process.
What you'll see in the OT Status column
✅
Approved
Employee submitted a request and you (or another supervisor) approved it. Extra hours will be credited.
🟡
Pending
Employee submitted a request that is waiting for your approval.
❌
Rejected
The request was denied. No extra hours are credited.
⚠️
No Approval
The system detected potential overtime but no request has been submitted. The employee should submit a request if the extra hours were authorized.
G-6
Auto Clock-Out
The system automatically closes sessions that have been open too long
🌐 All Companies ⚙️ Automatic 🔒 Supervisor Must Review

If an employee forgets to clock out and their session has been active for 20 hours or more, the system will automatically close it. This prevents runaway hours from accumulating and protects payroll accuracy.

When does this happen?

An employee clocked in but never clocked out, and more than 20 hours have passed since their clock-in time.

What does the system do?

The clock-out is set to the employee's scheduled shift end time — not to the time the system detected it. The record is flagged for mandatory review.

🔒 This record requires your action

Any record closed by auto clock-out cannot be submitted for payroll until a supervisor reviews and approves it. The row will appear with a purple tint and a clock icon (⏱) next to the employee's name.

What to do when you see this flag
🔍
Verify the hours
Check if the clock-out time recorded by the system (scheduled shift end) reflects reality. If the employee actually worked different hours, use the Edit button to correct it.
✅
Approve the record
Once you've verified the hours are correct, approve the record so it can move to payroll processing.
💬
Follow up with the employee
Consider reminding the employee to clock out at the end of their shift to avoid this situation.

D-1
Early Clock-In Confirmation
A prompt shown when an employee tries to clock in before the AM shift window
🏫 DayCare Only 👤 Employee-Facing

If an employee tries to clock in before the Driver/Aide AM shift begins (6:45 AM), the app shows a confirmation screen asking them to verify that the early clock-in is intentional. This prevents accidental early punches.

When does this happen?

An employee opens the clock-in screen before 6:45 AM on a day when they are scheduled for an AM Driver/Aide shift.

What the employee sees?

A prompt asking them to confirm that they are intentionally clocking in early. They must acknowledge it before the clock-in is recorded.

ℹ️ Note

This rule affects only the clock-in screen used by the employee. There is no flag or indicator visible to supervisors in the Punch Logs view unless something goes wrong after the early clock-in.

D-2
Driver/Aide Hour Distribution
How hours are split across AM, Regular, and PM segments
🏫 DayCare Only ⚙️ Automatic

For Driver/Aide employees, the system automatically allocates hours to the correct segment based on the employee's punch type. You do not need to do anything — this calculation is fully automatic.

The AM segment (1.25h) and Regular segment (5.5h) are always fixed — they never change regardless of when the employee actually clocked in or out. Only the PM segment varies, and its calculation is governed by D-3.

Where to find the breakdown
▶
Click the expand arrow on any Driver/Aide row
The expanded panel shows a breakdown of AM, Regular, and PM hours with the total. Fixed segments are labeled "fixed" and the PM segment shows "(actual)" if the employee left early.
D-3
PM 45-Minute Rule
Whether an employee gets full PM credit depends on how early they left
🏫 DayCare Only ⚙️ Automatic

The PM shift ends at 2:45 PM. Whether an employee receives the full 1.25h PM credit depends on how close to the end they clocked out. The idea is simple: if they stayed for most of the PM run, they get full credit; if they left much earlier, they only get credit for the time they actually worked.

When the employee clocked outPM Hours Given
Less than 45 minutes before 2:45 PM
(e.g., clocked out at 2:01 PM or later)		 Full 1.25h — complete PM credit
45 minutes or more before 2:45 PM
(e.g., clocked out at 2:00 PM or earlier)		 Actual time — only hours from 1:30 PM to clock-out
Session still active (not yet clocked out) Full 1.25h — full credit while active
⚠️ The 2:00 PM example

Clocking out at exactly 2:00 PM means the employee left 45 minutes before 2:45 PM — this triggers the actual time calculation, not full credit. Only departures after 2:00 PM (i.e., fewer than 45 minutes remaining) qualify for full PM credit.

When actual time is used, you will see "(actual)" in amber text next to the PM row in the expanded hours breakdown.

D-4
Regular Employee Late Clock-Out
A regular employee stayed past their shift end and into PM territory
🏫 DayCare Only 👁 Supervisor Review

Regular employees (non-Driver/Aide) are scheduled to clock out by 1:30 PM. If a regular employee clocks out significantly later than that — past the grace period — it may mean they accidentally stayed into PM time that belongs to a Driver/Aide employee's run. The system flags this so it can be reviewed.

When does this happen?

A Regular employee clocks out more than the grace period past their scheduled 1:30 PM end time — for example, clocking out at 1:50 PM with a 15-minute grace period.

What will you see?

The row is highlighted in red with a triangle warning icon (△) next to the employee's name. The expanded panel shows a D-4 explanation.

⚠️ PM hours are not credited

The system will not credit any extra PM hours to a Regular employee who triggers D-4. The clock-out time is capped at the scheduled shift end for payroll purposes. If the employee legitimately worked beyond 1:30 PM, a supervisor should review the record and adjust accordingly.

What to do when you see this flag
❓
Was the late clock-out intentional?
Did the employee have a reason to stay late? If so, review and approve. If not, this may indicate a forgotten clock-out or a mistake.
✏️
Correct if needed
Use the Edit button to adjust the clock-out time if the recorded time is incorrect.
D-5
Missing Clock-Out
An employee is still clocked in after their shift should have ended
🏫 DayCare Only 🔒 Action Required

If an employee is still showing as active (clocked in) after their scheduled shift end time has passed, the record is flagged as having a missing clock-out. This could mean the employee forgot to clock out, or there was a technical issue.

When does this happen?

The employee's session is still active and the current time is past their scheduled shift end. This is detected in real time.

What will you see?

The Status column shows an animated red "Missing Out" badge instead of the usual Active or Complete status.

🔒 Payroll is on hold

A record with a missing clock-out cannot be processed for payroll until a clock-out time is recorded. Either wait for the employee to clock out, or manually enter the correct time using the Edit button.

How to resolve this
📞
Contact the employee first
Ask if they are still working or if they forgot to clock out. This helps you enter the correct time.
✏️
Use the Edit button to enter the clock-out time
Click the pencil icon on the record, enter the correct clock-out time, and save. The flag will clear automatically once a clock-out is recorded.
